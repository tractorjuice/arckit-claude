# SFIA 8 Skills Reference

> Skills Framework for the Information Age (SFIA) - Version 8
> Reference for G-Cloud Lot 3 (Cloud Support) submissions

## Overview

SFIA provides a common language for describing skills in IT and digital. G-Cloud Lot 3 (Cloud Support) services should map capabilities to SFIA skills.

## Skill Levels

| Level | Title | Autonomy | Influence | Complexity | Business Skills |
|-------|-------|----------|-----------|------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Follow | Works under supervision | Minimal | Routine | Developing |
| 2 | Assist | Works under routine direction | Individual | Straightforward | Basic |
| 3 | Apply | Works under general direction | Local team | Varied | Competent |
| 4 | Enable | Works autonomously | Project/team | Complex | Mature |
| 5 | Ensure/Advise | Broad autonomy | Organisation | Challenging | Strong |
| 6 | Initiate/Influence | Full autonomy | Significant | Highly complex | Expert |
| 7 | Set Strategy/Inspire | Accountable | Strategic | Most complex | Authority |

## UK Government AI Skills Framework

> Complementary framework for AI workforce capabilities
> Reference: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ai-skills-for-the-uk-workforce

### AI Skills Overview

The UK Government AI Skills Framework defines competencies across **3 domains** and **3 job levels**, designed to complement SFIA for AI-enabled services.

### AI Skill Domains

| Domain | Focus | SFIA Alignment |
|--------|-------|----------------|
| **Technical AI** | Operating, monitoring, guiding AI systems | MLNG, DTAN, PROG |
| **Responsible & Ethical AI** | Ethics, transparency, bias, regulation | INAS, SCTY, GOVN |
| **Non-Technical AI** | Understanding, evaluating, adopting AI | CNSL, BUAN, CHMG |

### AI Levels Mapped to SFIA

| AI Level | SFIA Equivalent | Autonomy | Typical Role |
|----------|-----------------|----------|--------------|
| **Entry** | Level 2-3 | Individual AI tool use | AI-Assisted Analyst |
| **Mid** | Level 4-5 | Guide teams, embed AI in workflows | AI Implementation Lead |
| **Managerial** | Level 6-7 | Organisational AI decisions | AI Strategy Director |

---

### AI Skills by Level

#### Entry-Level AI Skills (SFIA 2-3)

| Domain | Competencies |
|--------|--------------|
| **Technical** | Write prompts for AI tools; Operate embedded AI features (autocomplete, transcription); Perform routine tasks using AI |
| **Responsible** | Assess accuracy of AI outputs; Identify bias in AI results; Apply data privacy guidance; Assess risks in AI decisions |
| **Non-Technical** | Use basic AI tools; Test AI for task suitability; Seek support when needed; Report observations to colleagues |

#### Mid-Level AI Skills (SFIA 4-5)

| Domain | Competencies |
|--------|--------------|
| **Technical** | Use AI in job-specific workflows; Apply low-code AI automation; Create dashboards/tools using AI; Integrate AI into role-specific tasks |
| **Responsible** | Evaluate AI content for accuracy; Apply corrective measures for bias; Guide ethical decisions in teams; Apply responsible AI policies |
| **Non-Technical** | Coordinate AI use with colleagues; Provide feedback on AI tools; Support peer learning; Use AI insights to improve services |

#### Managerial-Level AI Skills (SFIA 6-7)

| Domain | Competencies |
|--------|--------------|
| **Technical** | Manage AI integration into services; Manage AI-supported automation; Monitor AI use across functions |
| **Responsible** | Define accountability for AI use; Manage GDPR/data ethics compliance; Apply equity and transparency principles; Assess long-term AI risks |
| **Non-Technical** | Align AI with team objectives; Plan AI strategy; Train staff on AI tools; Evaluate scalability and ROI; Guide responsible AI adoption |

---

### AI Skills Adoption Pathway

| Stage | Activity | Outcome |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1. Awareness | Encounter AI through news, peers, events | Initial understanding |
| 2. Exploration | Identify where AI offers value | Use case identification |
| 3. Assessment | Evaluate existing AI skills and readiness | Gap analysis |
| 4. Experimentation | Test AI tools in low-risk settings | Proof of concept |
| 5. Reflection | Review experiences, identify lessons | Learning capture |
| 6. Upskilling | Structured training for competency | Capability building |
| 7. Integration | Embed proven tools into processes | Operational use |
| 8. Strategy | Align AI use with priorities | Strategic alignment |
| 9. Scaling | Extend AI across teams/services | Organisation-wide adoption |
